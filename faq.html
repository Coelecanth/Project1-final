About Us

The fly fishing Lodge is a small Family business. We fish the Kumquatcha River which is about 100 miles north of Anchorage. We have a number of fly fishing lodges on the Kumquatcha River. Our lodges are located from where the river empties in to the sea, to the higher reaches of the river. Each lodge offers a different experience in terms of location and fly fishing.

The Fly Fishing Lodge operates in co-operation with native Inuit Indians resident on the Kumquatcha River, and we operate our lodges on zero impact to the environment.

Please see our reviews on Social media (see the links below) to see how our guests enjoyed and loved staying with us, and see some of the great fish they caught.

Want to know more get in contact with us through contact page, and join our newsletter for regular news on the destination and the fishing.

Accomadation

We pride ourselves in operatimg a premium quality, low impact destination for the fly fisher, staying in comforatable accomadation, the lodges we operate offer differnt locations along the river and each one has its own personality.

You stay in log cabin accomdation, which provide comfortable accomadation for two and a great place to rest after a day on the river. There are drying facilities for things like waders and coats, for those days when you need to dry out.

All drinks food and amenties are provided as part of the price, whether on the river bank or in the lodge. We always strive to make sure our guests have a great time.

The Wilderness

The lodge offers fly fishers that once in a lifetime chance to visit a true wilderness and fish in a truly wild and untouched environment, where the wild life is all around you.

We have Beavers, and Bald Eagles, and there are lots of other wild life to see, all withing minutes of the lodges.

While out on the river you will see the variety this landscape has to offer and our expert guides are there to help you see it.

Come and Catch Fish!!

The best time to visit Alaskan Fly Lodge is between the months of June and August. The peak of the salmon run is between late June and mid July, During this time we have the plentiful runs of King salmon, Chum salmon, and Sockeye salmon. |

We also have the native fish species in the river. The "Dolly Varden" the native river Arctic Char, and the 'Leopard' Rainbow Trout and Grayling, all of which can be caught on "the fly".

Later in the season we have Silver salmon or Coho. We also have runs of the sea run Arctic Char as well. In addition ot this every other year we have runs of Pink (humpback) salmon in the river.

What to Bring With You

The weather in Alaska can vary a lot, so even June/July rain is not unexpected. For this reason should bring layers of clothing to suit from warm to cool temperatures. Waterproof jacket is essential and for fishing in the river a pair of chest waders is also a must.

For most comfortable of the time causal clothes are all that are quired.

On very warm still days Mosquitos can be problem so bring plenty of repellent and something for bites. The lodges also have there own facilities to tie flies as well. Once your visit is booked with us, we can provide a comprehensive list of all the things you need, so don’t worry we have it covered for you.

With regard to fishing tackle we can provide a comprehensive list of things you will need. We are also able to offer loan of equipment if you have forgotten something.

Getting to the Fly Fishing Lodge

Depart from your preferred airport and arrive in Anchorage, AK. Prior to travel, you will need to advise us of your travel arrangements, so a host from the Fly fishing Lodge will meet you at the airport.

Depending on your iterary, you can either stay the night in Anchorage and the fly out in the morning or travel on to your Lodge. We can assist with accomdation and transport for your over night stay in Anchorage.

If you are travelling on, our Fly Fishing Lodge host will assist you to the next part of your adventure, where you will fly in a float plane onto your final destination, on the river.

The flight to Kumquatcha River is roughly 50 minutes and offers breathtaking views of the scenery. Upon your arrival at the river, a Fly Fishing Lodge host will be there to greet you at the the dock.

From here you and your luggage will then be transferred to one of the fishing skiffs, and will then make the scenic journey up river, to arrive at your chosen lodge. Where a hearty welcome and introduction will be awaiting to make your stay with us as comfortable and enjoyable as possible.

On your return journey, you will return to Anchoraage, and we provide transit to Anchorage as part of the pacakge for you. After this you can return home or extend your stay in Anchorage and we would be happy to assit you with this.
